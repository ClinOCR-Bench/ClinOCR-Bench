You have undergone a colonoscopy with intravenous sedation. The medication will remain in your system for several hours, affecting your judgment and physical coordination. For this reason, you must be escorted home by a responsible adult who can also stay with you.
For the next 24 hours, your safety depends on following these rules:
Do not drive a car or operate any type of machinery. Your reaction time is slowed, making these activities dangerous.
Do not consume any alcoholic beverages. Alcohol can have an adverse interaction with the sedatives you were given.
Do not make any important decisions or sign any legally binding papers.
Rest and relax for the remainder of the day. You can return to your normal routine tomorrow.
It is common to feel bloated and experience gas or mild cramping after the procedure. This is normal and should resolve as you pass the air that was used during the exam. Walking can help this process. You may start with clear liquids and light foods (like soup or toast) and advance to your regular diet as you feel comfortable.
