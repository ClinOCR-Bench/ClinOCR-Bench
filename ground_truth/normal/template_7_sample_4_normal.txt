The patient is a 22-year-old male who presented for a routine skin examination. He noted a mole on his upper back that he believes has become slightly darker over the past year. He denies any symptoms of pain or itching associated with the lesion. He has a history of significant sun exposure during his teen years and reports occasional sunburns.
PROCEDURES:
Shave Biopsy, Upper Back: A 5mm pigmented macule was identified on the superior aspect of the right scapula. After obtaining consent, the area was anesthetized with 1% lidocaine with epinephrine, and a deep shave biopsy of the lesion was performed. Hemostasis was achieved with aluminum chloride. The specimen was sent for histopathologic examination.
IMPRESSION:
MICROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION: Sections show a symmetric, well-circumscribed proliferation of nested and single melanocytes confined to the dermal-epidermal junction. The melanocytes are uniform in size and shape with no significant cytologic atypia. No mitotic activity or ulceration is identified.
DIAGNOSIS: Junctional melanocytic nevus, benign.
