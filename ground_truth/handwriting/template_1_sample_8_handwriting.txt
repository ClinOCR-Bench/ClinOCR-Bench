Polysomnography Report
Procedure: Attended, full-night diagnostic polysomnography.
Clinical Indication: Excessive daytime sleepiness, reported snoring, and witnessed apneas.
This was a technically adequate overnight diagnostic polysomnogram. Total sleep time was 345 minutes with a sleep efficiency of 82%. Sleep architecture was abnormal due to significant sleep fragmentation. REM sleep was reduced, comprising only 10% of total sleep time.
Frequent respiratory events were noted throughout the night. The overall Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI) was 42 events per hour, which is in the severe range. These events were predominantly obstructive apneas and hypopneas, and they were more severe during REM sleep. This was associated with significant oxygen desaturation, with a nadir SpO2 of 78%. The patient's baseline oxygen saturation was 95%. No significant periodic limb movements were noted.
Impression: The findings are consistent with severe obstructive sleep apnea (OSA).
A follow-up appointment is recommended to discuss treatment options, including positive airway pressure (PAP) therapy. A subsequent PAP titration study under polysomnography is indicated to determine the optimal pressure for treatment.
